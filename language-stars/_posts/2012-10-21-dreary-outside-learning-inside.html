We've spent the weekend trapped inside looking at the rain, and even when it stops it's just cold and gray outside.  So when our little ones announce they want to go to the park, it's not really an option. Fortunately we had a few things to do this weekend, such as a double-shot of Lanaguage Stars.

Not to criticize outdoor activities, but it's nice to have someplace to take Leilani that doesn't shut down in bad weather.  It's also great that she's taking the time to learn, rather than sitting around watching TV.  With the winter ahead it's a good time to think of some activities we can engage in with the kids when we're all stuck indoors.

Games

Simple board games are always a fun diversion during the winter season where the entire family can get involved. With a child Leilani's age the games need to be simple, but fun that everybody wants to play.  Toy stores tend to market games with tons of little pieces, cards and a bunch of rules.  Children find them attractive at the store because they've got pictures of princesses or cartoon characters on them, but the best games are the classics.  Leili is particularly partial to memory games, and look here's one in Spanish.

Drawing

It's not so great for her language development, but my little girl is in love with dot-to-dot puzzles that her grandmother bought her at the moment.  It's great for her counting and hand-eye coordination.

Stories

Leilani always likes a good book, and she also loves correcting my Spanish pronunciation.  Longer stories can be a family activity, read together.

Get Outside When You Can

Finally when the weather starts to get bad it can be habitual to stay indoors.  When I started this blog it was gray and dreary, but the weather has turned beautiful.  Take advantage!  Get outside.
